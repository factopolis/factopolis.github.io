Amazon Doesn't Pay Sales Tax

The Truth

It collects sales tax in all states which have one, plus income tax, property tax, payroll tax, etc..

    • And as of this April, Amazon has collected sales taxes in all states that have one. (Five states — Alaska, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire and Oregon — do not.) Amazon does not, however, require third-party sellers on its marketplace to collect sales taxes, but offers the service to them.

    Amazon Has a Monopoly
    Amazon is gigantic, but not a monopoly.

    Fact Checks

    • Amazon is an industry giant, but it does not exert enough market control to be considered a monopoly, and no U.S. tribunal has ever deemed it as such.

      While Amazon takes advantage of tax breaks and loopholes, it pays federal corporate tax, and charges sales taxes in 46 U.S. jurisdictions. It also supports federal legislation that would require other online retailers to pay state tax on internet sales.

      PolitiFact
