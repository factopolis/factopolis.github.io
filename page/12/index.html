  11. And one of the points I want to make is, is that if you actually took the number of Muslims Americans, we’d be one of the largest Muslim countries in the world.
    Barack Obama, President of the United States of America (2009 - 2017)
    Interview With Laurence Haïm of CANAL+
    YouTube ( 2:25 - 2:37 ) Transcript (obamawhitehouse.archives.gov)
    on Jun 1, 2009

    Claims

    America Is One of the Largest Muslim Countries
    Estimates are difficult, but somewhere between 58th and 29th place.

    Fact Checks

    • Ranking 58 out of the world’s 60 most populous Muslim nations does not a large Muslim country make. Indeed, by even the most generous estimate we found of 8 million, the United States still ranks 29 out of 60.
      PolitiFact

    • The ARIS survey did not attempt to estimate the number of Muslim children, as the Pew Research Center did. So we judge Pew’s 2.35 million figure to be the best available estimate of the total U.S. Muslim population.

      That’s also the figure used by the Wikipedia ranking of countries with the largest Muslim populations, which puts the U.S. at No. 52. There are by most counts 195 countries in the world, so the U.S. would be in the top half, though not in the top one-quarter.

      FactCheck.org
  12. And 100 percent, John, of your ads — 100 percent of them have been negative.
    Barack Obama, President of the United States of America (2009 - 2017)
    3rd Presidential Debate
    YouTube ( 26:48 - 26:54 ) Transcript (NYTimes.com)
    on Oct 15, 2008

    Claims

    All of John McCain's 2008 Campaign Ads Were Negative
    Only about 73% were.

    Fact Checks

    • But McCain has aired many, many ads that were not negative. If you look at a report from the same organization on Sept. 17, they found that in the week after the conventions, for example, Obama aired a higher percentage of negative ads than did McCain (76 percent to 56 percent).

      In all, the Wisconsin Advertising Project has found that 73 percent of McCain’s ads have been negative, to date. That’s far short of 100 percent. (It also found 61 percent of Obama’s ads have been negative.)

      PolitiFact
  13. I remember landing under sniper fire.
    Hillary Clinton, United States Secretary of State (2009 - 2013)
    Speech on Iraq Ware at George Washington University
    C-SPAN ( 9:38 - 9:40 )
    on Mar 17, 2008
    Retracted on Mar 24, 2008

    Claims

    Hillary Clinton Landed in Under Sniper in 1996 in Tuzla, Bosnia
    It didn’t happen.

    Fact Checks

    • There’s no doubt flying into Bosnia was dangerous back in 1996, but the threat of sniper fire is not the same as actual sniper fire, and hustling off the tarmac is not the same as running with your head down. Yes, Clinton later acknowledged that she was mistaken, but it’s hard to understand how she could err on something so significant as whether she did or didn’t dodge sniper bullets. Quite simply, this kind of hyperbole deserves our harshest assessment. We rule Pants on Fire.
      PolitiFact
  14. Last summer he basically threatened to bomb Pakistan, which I don’t think was a particularly wise position to take.
    Hillary Clinton, United States Secretary of State (2009 - 2013)
    Democratic Presidential Candidates Debate
    C-SPAN ( 37:00 - 37:09 )
    on Feb 26, 2008

    Claims

    Obama Basically Threatened to Bomb Pakistan
    He didn’t.

It’s not enough to call that False. We called it Pants-on-Fire wrong when McCain said it and we’re doing the same for Clinton.
      PolitiFact
