    Claims

    The Estate Tax Strongly Affects Farmers
    Very few farms are large enough to qualify for the estate tax.

    Fact Checks

    • That assertion appears to be at odds with statistics. Only 1.7 percent of family-farm estates were required to file an estate-tax return in 2016 and only 0.4 percent ended up owing the tax, according to an analysis by the Agriculture Department.     NFL Ratings Way Down Due to Player Protests

    Fact Checks

    • In a world where TV viewing is increasingly fragmented and more people are watching on devices or saving programs until later, the majority of TV shows have smaller ratings than the year before. Still, pro football games remain among the most-watched things on television.

  • Because right now, we are one of the highest-taxed nations in the world.
    Donald Trump, President of the United States of America
    Rally in Cedar Rapids, IA
    YouTube ( 19:01 - 19:11 ) Transcript (Factba.se)
    on Jun 21, 2017

    Claims

    The US Has Highest Tax Rate in the World
    It doesn’t.
    1. John Cornyn

    Fact Checks

    • The overall U.S. tax burden is actually one of the lowest among the 32 developed and large emerging-market economies tracked by the Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development.

      Taxes made up 26.4 percent of the total U.S. economy in 2015, according to the OECD. That’s far below Denmark’s tax burden of 46.6 percent, Britain’s 32.5 percent or Germany’s 36.9 percent. Just four OECD countries had a lower tax bite than the U.S.: South Korea, Ireland, Chile and Mexico.

      Associated Press

  • We strongly support NATO, an alliance forged through the bonds of two world wars that dethroned fascism, and a Cold War, and defeated communism.

    But our partners must meet their financial obligations. And now, based on our very strong and frank discussions, they are beginning to do just that.

    Donald Trump, President of the United States of America
    Speech to Joint Session of Congress
    YouTube ( 52:51 - 53:30 ) Transcript (WhiteHouse.gov) Transcript (CNN.com)
    on Feb 28, 2017

    Claims

    Trump Got NATO Partners to Increase Defense Spending
    NATO countries agreed to meet defense spending requirements in 2014.

    Fact Checks

    • No new money has come pouring in from NATO allies. Defense Secretary Jim Mattis made a strong case when he met with allied defense ministers at a NATO gathering last month, pressing them to meet their 2014 commitment to spend 2 percent of their gross domestic product on defense by 2024. Mattis and other leaders said the allies understood the message and there was some discussion about working out plans to meet the goal.

      Only five of the 28 member countries currently meet the 2 percent level, and no new commitments have been made since the NATO meeting. Others in the alliance have routinely said they will work toward the increase. In any event, the commitment is for these nations to spend more on their own military capabilities, which would strengthen the alliance, not to hand over money.

      Associated Press
